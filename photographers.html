David Neilson

David Neilson is an award-winning photographer based in Bristol, in the United Kingdom. David has won the “One-Shot” award in 2011, 2012, 2014 and 2016 and has been nominated for “Best photo journalist” in 2020. His iconic urban landscapes, known for their dystopian themes bring a level of depth, texture and emotion to each scene. He began touring with artists in early 2012, hitting the road with bands like “Architects”, “Harmonix” and “EMP Babies” and has continued to be involved with the live music scene ever since. Whether on the road or in the studio, David can capture those important moments and truly bring your band’s history to a new level.

Jessica Jorgen

Originally hailing from Norway, Jessica Jorgan, now based in Paris, is the all-time winner of Aperture’s “Golden lens” award not just once, but over three successive years. With a history of fine art and graphic design, Jessica has carried her skill for creating a sense of mystery to any scene. Using various techniques, she has developer herself, Jessica has produced many dreamlike scenes varying from dark surrealism to soft, psychedelic-feeling masterpieces. With a serious passion for music, Jessica became involved with the scene in 2015 when she did a series of cover-shoots for the seminal rock band “Metaphysix”. From there, she went on to tour with many artists such as “Blame machine”, “BOBA”, “The Astral Tones” and many, many more. No project is too abstract for this master of the photography world. Her painterly style brings band photography to another dimension.
